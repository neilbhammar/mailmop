# How to Stop Booking.com Emails

**Quick answer:** To stop Booking.com emails, open a recent Booking.com email and unsubscribe, then go to your Booking.com notification settings at https://www.booking.com/subscription_manager.html and switch off the categories you don't want. Booking.com controls its email by category, so one unsubscribe click usually stops only a single type. To clear the backlog, search `from:booking.com` in Gmail and bulk delete.

## What Booking.com actually sends you

Booking.com sends reservation emails (confirmations, modifications, reminders), marketing and promotions (deals, Genius rewards, surveys), review invitations, unfinished booking follow-ups, and administrative and security alerts. Its email comes from `booking.com`. The reason your inbox fills up is that these are often separate lists or notification types, so switching off one doesn't stop the rest.

## How to stop Booking.com emails, step by step

1. **Unsubscribe from a recent email.** Open a recent Booking.com email and use its unsubscribe link. 
2. **Turn it off at the source.** Open Booking.com's email or notification settings at [https://www.booking.com/subscription_manager.html](https://www.booking.com/subscription_manager.html) (Sign in > account settings > Manage your communication preferences (or click Unsubscribe at the bottom of a marketing email)) and turn off the categories you don't want.

## Does unsubscribing from Booking.com actually work?

Booking.com email comes in two separate streams. The unsubscribe link in a marketing email opts you out of marketing only (deals, Genius, surveys, newsletters). Reservation and service email (booking confirmations, modifications, reminders, security) has no user opt-out and keeps arriving while you have an active reservation. To stop marketing reliably, click Unsubscribe in a marketing email and set communication preferences in your logged-in account, since the email link alone is sometimes reported as ineffective. Marketing is managed per channel (email, push, SMS, in-app), so turning off email does not stop app push. That's the part most "how to unsubscribe" guides miss, and it's why Booking.com email often keeps arriving after people think they've opted out.

## Clear out the Booking.com emails already in your inbox

Unsubscribing stops new mail. It doesn't remove what's already there. On desktop Gmail, search `from:booking.com`, click the select-all checkbox, then click **"Select all conversations that match this search"**, and hit the trash icon. Empty Trash to reclaim the storage.

## A couple of Booking.com-specific tips

- Don't rely on the email link alone. Also set preferences inside the logged-in account, and confirm you are in the correct account.
- Booking.com markets through email, push, SMS, and in-app separately, so check the mobile app's notification settings too. Expect reservation confirmations to continue.
